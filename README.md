## How to Read a Kratom Shot Label

### Mitragynine Content

This should be listed in milligrams per bottle.

Legitimate products specify exact amounts. Vague descriptions like "high potency extract" without quantification signal a weak or unverified product.

**Look for:** `150mg mitragynine per 15ml` or similar specific language.

---

### Extraction Ratio

Some shots list extraction ratios — 10:1, 15:1, 25:1.

This means the product used 10, 15, or 25 grams of raw leaf per gram of extract produced.

Higher ratios indicate more concentrated product. But alkaloid content varies between leaf batches — extraction ratio alone doesn't guarantee potency. Mitragynine mg content per bottle is more reliable.

---

### Ingredient Transparency

Clean shots list simple ingredients.

Kratom extract, purified water, citric acid, natural flavor — this is a clean label.

Proprietary blend language, undisclosed "botanical extracts," or long ingredient lists with unrecognizable compounds are immediate red flags.

---

### Batch Number and COA Access

Premium brands include batch numbers on shot bottles.

This allows cross-referencing with published COAs on their website. You can verify that your specific bottle matches tested alkaloid content.

This level of documentation is the gold standard in 2026.
